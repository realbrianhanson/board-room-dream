# Concept Brief — Stocks For Kids

## The transformation

A kid goes from "the stock market is a confusing grown-up thing" to "I own a
piece of Disney, I know why I bought it, and I can explain what happened to
it this week." Real market data makes the stakes feel real; fake money makes
the mistakes free; a real teacher makes the lessons stick.

## The users (three, and all three matter)

1. **The kid (8–14)** — the user. Gets $10,000 of fake money (teacher can
   adjust), a market of ~50 companies they actually recognize, and a
   portfolio that moves with the real market. Every trade requires a
   one-line "why" — the reflection IS the curriculum.
2. **The teacher (Courtney)** — the driver. Runs a classroom, posts lessons
   ("Coach's Corner"), sees every kid's portfolio and every trade note,
   resets/adjusts balances, and hands out shout-outs. The dashboard passes
   the Courtney test: understandable in 10 seconds, no explaining required.
3. **The parent** — the account holder and the customer of record. Creates
   the account, creates the kid's profile, joins the classroom by code, and
   can see everything their kid does. Parent consent gates everything
   (COPPA posture).

## The 3 core jobs

1. **Trade for real (without the real).** Browse a curated market with live
   (delayed) prices, buy and sell fractional shares at the server's price,
   watch the portfolio move with the actual market.
2. **Learn out loud.** Every trade captures a reason; lessons from the
   teacher land in the kid's app; badges reward behavior (first trade,
   diversified, held through a dip, wrote a great reason) — never raw
   returns.
3. **Teach with visibility.** The teacher sees the whole classroom at a
   glance — who traded, what they said, who's stuck — and steers the next
   lesson from real activity, not homework guesses.

## Why real data (and what "real" means here)

Delayed quotes (15 min) from a real provider. Kids don't day-trade; the
lesson is ownership, patience, and reasoning — delayed data is
indistinguishable from live for that purpose and keeps the app inside free
API tiers and educational data-license terms. Prices, charts, and the
portfolio math are all genuinely real. The only fake thing is the money.
